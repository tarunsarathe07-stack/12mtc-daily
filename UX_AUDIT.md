# UX Audit: 12 Minutes Daily

## Landing

Severity: High

The page explains the concept but still relies on long copy and a boxed demo. A first-time visitor should understand the loop in five seconds: read 12 cards, learn the CLAT angle, take 12 questions, protect the streak.

Fix: tighten hero copy, make the product loop visually dominant, use saffron for the primary action, and reduce generic card repetition.

## Login / Signup

Severity: Medium

The forms work, but the visual treatment feels less premium than the rest of the app. Error text is clear, but the page lacks a reassuring reason to create an account.

Fix: keep auth simple, add one short benefit line, stronger focus states, and a clearer password reset path.

## Today

Severity: High

Today is the most important screen. It has the right information, but sections compete: progress, demo notice, quiz CTA, queue, quick links, weak topics. The page should feel like a daily command center with one obvious next action.

Fix: make the Today's 12 progress strip and next action dominant; make demo mode compact; convert repeated cards into rows, rails, and compact modules.

## Shorts

Severity: High

The deck mechanics are strong, but visual noise comes from random topic colors and dense card content. The "why it matters" area must never clip and should be expandable by default.

Fix: use indigo/saffron badges, images when available, branded topic art fallback, compact source/date/trust row, scroll-safe card body, and strong empty states.

## Daily Study Note

Severity: Medium

The structure is useful, but some sections still look like generic app cards. The page should feel like a premium study note with visible hierarchy.

Fix: strengthen the trust strip, use editorial separators, and keep sticky actions clear.

## Quiz Battle

Severity: Medium

This is currently one of the strongest parts. It should remain focused and dark, but the timer and score race can use saffron more consistently.

Fix: light refinement only; do not overhaul.

## Results

Severity: Medium

The result page needs to close the loop: rank, XP, weak topic, share, revise. Avoid over-celebrating when the score is poor.

Fix: make weak-topic revision and share card more visible.

## Blog / Guides

Severity: Medium

The blog is improving and correctly separated from current affairs. It needs stronger publication rhythm and clearer conversion without looking salesy.

Fix: featured guide, category sections, sticky conversion CTA, and better excerpt rhythm.

## Archive

Severity: Low

The archive is clear, but topic badges can inherit random colors.

Fix: use restrained brand tags and improve empty state.

## Profile

Severity: Medium

Profile still has the most generic dashboard feel: many cards, stats, separators, and badges. It is acceptable for now but should later be redesigned as a progress identity page.

Fix later: replace card grid with a progress timeline, streak calendar, saved cards, and clear conversion CTA.

## Cross-Cutting Issues

- Repeated white cards make the app feel template-built.
- Topic badge colors drift away from the brand.
- Some empty states exist but are too plain.
- Keyboard navigation works in Shorts, but more focus states should be visible.
- Mobile card content must stay scroll-safe.
